# QuantumQuote — packaging & pricing model (v1 draft)

*Working draft for Shawn — the one-pager to sell against Quote IQ and DealHub.
Numbers are starting points for discussion, not commitments.*

## What we're selling
HubSpot-native CPQ for copier/MFD dealers: configurator with a real rules
engine, margin guardrails with manager approval, lease rate cards, service
agreements (per-device or pooled), trade-up buyouts, and grouped writeback to
the HubSpot deal. The pitch in one line: **"Quote IQ, but it lives where your
pipeline already lives — HubSpot — and you keep control of your data."**

## Competitive price anchors
| Product | Model | Street price |
|---|---|---|
| KeyPoint Quote IQ | per-seat SaaS + implementation | undisclosed; bundled with KeyPoint relationship |
| DealHub | per-seat SaaS | ~$78–$1,000/user/mo depending on tier |
| Agent Dealer (Salesforce) | per-org + Salesforce tax | high; requires Salesforce licenses |

The buyer is a dealer principal or sales VP who already pays for HubSpot.
Their alternative cost is 2–3 platforms; our wedge is consolidation.

## Recommended structure: per-portal base + seat bands
- **Base platform fee per dealer (portal): $499/mo** — includes 5 quoting
  seats, the admin console, rate cards, rules engine, HubSpot sync.
- **Additional seats: $59/seat/mo** in bands of 5.
- **Implementation & onboarding: $2,500 one-time** — catalog import, floor
  costs, rate sheets, rules setup, 2 training sessions. (This is the QBS
  services motion — it's also the relationship builder.)
- **Optional add-ons** (later): e-sign volume, meter integrations
  (FMAudit/PrintFleet), multi-brand white-label.

Why per-portal-first instead of pure per-seat: dealers have lumpy seat counts
and hate per-seat creep (it's the #1 DealHub complaint); a flat base makes the
CFO conversation easy and the seat band still scales revenue with big shops.

## Rule-of-thumb unit economics for the buyer
A dealer doing 30 deals/mo at ~$28k average equipment: if the tool lifts
blended margin 1 point (better guardrails, fewer fat-finger discounts), that's
~$8.4k/mo in recovered GP — 9–17× the subscription. Lead the pitch with this.
